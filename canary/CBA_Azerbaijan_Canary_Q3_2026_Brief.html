◈ The Azerbaijan Canary — Unique Mechanism

BTC/SCP Single Trigger (KC_01)

The Baku-Tbilisi-Ceyhan (BTC) and South Caucasus Pipeline (SCP) are the ONLY major Caspian hydrocarbon export routes that bypass both Russia and Iran. There is no redundant route. BTC (1,768km) connects Baku to the Turkish Mediterranean terminal at Ceyhan, carrying ~800k-1.1M bbl/day of Azerbaijani and Kazakh crude. SCP (692km) runs parallel to deliver 25+ bcm/year of Shah Deniz gas to Turkey and Europe.

Threat vectors: PKK bombed BTC in 2008 (Turkey). IRGC has demonstrated precision capability. The 2020/2023 Azerbaijan wars established drone (Bayraktar TB2) strike accuracy — a drone strike on exposed pipeline infrastructure is now a credible threat. A geological event (earthquake along North Anatolian fault) is a secondary risk.

AZN Peg & SOFAZ Architecture (KC_02)

The Azerbaijani manat (AZN) is pegged to the USD at 1.70, maintained by the State Oil Fund (SOFAZ) with $54B+ in assets (as of Q3 2026). The peg is not defended by CBA FX intervention alone — SOFAZ's oil-revenue reserve is the backstop.

Devaluation history: Azerbaijan devalued AZN by 21% (February 2015) and 32% (December 2015) when Brent oil crashed below $50/bbl. The 2015 playbook: Azeri Light below $55/bbl for 3+ months + SOFAZ drawdown below $40B = peg at risk. Current Azeri Light at ~$75/bbl and SOFAZ at $54B means the peg is comfortably intact in 2026.

Post-Karabakh South Caucasus Order (KC_03)

Azerbaijan's September 2023 military operation (24-hour) restored full sovereignty over Nagorno-Karabakh. ~100,000 ethnic Armenians evacuated. The conflict is resolved, but border normalization is ongoing along the full 1,000km Armenia-Azerbaijan border. The Zangezur corridor — Azerbaijan's demand for an overland route through southern Armenia to link the Azerbaijani exclave of Nakhchivan — is the remaining flashpoint.

A new military incident or peace talks collapse would shatter the investment climate and could trigger secondary BTC/SCP security concerns.

SOCAR / ACG Production Architecture (KC_05)

SOCAR (State Oil Company of Azerbaijan) holds ~$6B in international debt (Eurobonds, project finance). ACG (Azeri-Chirag-Gunashli) is the flagship Caspian field, operated by BP with partners including Equinor, ExxonMobil, and SOCAR. ACG is past peak production (~600k bbl/day in 2010 → ~415k bbl/day in 2026). The production decline is gradual but structurally irreversible without major new investment.

SOCAR Eurobond spread at ~280bps (2026) is not stressed. Spread above 600bps would signal KC_05 escalation. SOCAR's refinery/petrochemical diversification (Star refinery in Turkey) provides partial hedge against ACG decline.
