◈ Executive Summary

The Dominican Republic's DRRI (Dominican Republic Aggregate Risk Index) sits at 0.3650 WATCH as of Q3 2026, reflecting elevated but contained structural stress anchored by Haiti's catastrophic state collapse. The Dominican Republic occupies the eastern two-thirds of Hispaniola — an island it shares with Haiti, the Western Hemisphere's most complete state failure. The SINGLE TRIGGER Kill Condition KC_01 — Haiti State Collapse / Mass Migration Surge — is the unique mechanism that makes this canary unlike any other in CivilisationOS: the threat is entirely caused by an adjacent foreign failed state's gang warfare, not domestic policy error.

The DOP (Dominican peso, managed float) remains stable at ~58/USD, below the 60 alert. Diaspora remittances of $9B/year (~8% GDP) represent the second transmission channel. The DR is Latin America's fastest-growing economy (avg 5.8% GDP growth 2015-2024) — Haiti instability is the existential threat to this trajectory.

◈ Structural Analysis — Dominican Republic Q3 2026

KC_01 SINGLE TRIGGER: Haiti State Collapse

Haiti is the Western Hemisphere's most complete state failure. The Viv Ansanm coalition (Vitye Viv Ansanm — "Let's Live Together") formed in 2023 by merging the G9 and GPEP gang federations. The coalition now controls approximately 85% of Port-au-Prince, including key infrastructure: ports, fuel depots, and main highways. The DR's response has been unprecedented: a 200km border barrier (concrete + steel), 15,000+ military deployed to the frontier, mass deportation operations (250k in 2023, 300k+ in 2024), and suspension of the Dajabon River water treaty in 2024. The SINGLE TRIGGER threshold — 500,000 new migrants within 6 months — would overwhelm DR deportation infrastructure, create a humanitarian crisis in border provinces (Dajabón, Monte Cristi, Independencia), and trigger a political emergency in Santo Domingo.

KC_02: DOP / Tourism Revenue Shock

The Dominican Republic is the Caribbean's largest tourism destination: ~10 million visitors in 2025, generating $9B+ in direct tourism revenue. Punta Cana (eastern DR) is the primary resort hub with 70,000+ hotel rooms. Tourism generates 8% of GDP directly and 15% when indirect effects are counted. The 'Hispaniola danger' narrative risk is real: media conflation of Haiti gang violence with DR safety creates booking hesitation. Royal Caribbean's Labadee beach closure would be a visible signal. The DOP (Dominican peso) is a managed float: BCRD maintains the rate through FX intervention. USDDOP above 60 signals stress; above 70 is a critical deterioration that would increase import costs and fuel inflation.

KC_03: MSS Mission — Kenya's Haiti Gamble

The UN-mandated Multinational Security Support mission represents a novel security architecture: a non-permanent-member-led stabilization force funded primarily by the US ($300M committed). Kenya deployed approximately 1,000 police officers starting January 2024. The mission has struggled to make progress against well-armed gangs with superior local intelligence and urban terrain advantage. Viv Ansanm has continued territorial expansion despite MSS presence. A Kenya withdrawal — triggered by casualty pressure, funding gaps, or political fatigue — would be the clearest MSS failure signal. This would remove the only international security architecture between Haiti and permanent failed-state status. Consequence: DR faces indefinite DRRI elevation from KC_01.

KC_04: CAFTA-DR & Remittance Architecture

The Dominican Republic-Central America Free Trade Agreement (CAFTA-DR) provides duty-free access to the US market for DR textiles, apparel, tobacco, and sugar. The DR is the 15th largest US trading partner. The garment sector (free trade zones) employs 160,000 workers. The diaspora remittance channel — $9B/year from 2M+ Dominicans in the US — is the second-largest foreign exchange earner after tourism. Western Union dominates the corridor. US Congressional pressure related to DR treatment of Haitian migrants could weaponize CAFTA-DR review as political leverage. Should tariff threats materialize, HBI, PVH, VFC, and DOLE face sourcing disruption. The DR's sugar quota (historically contested with US producers) is a separate lever.

The Dominican Paradox: Growth Miracle Next to State Failure

The DR has been Latin America's fastest-growing economy for nearly a decade (avg 5.8% GDP growth 2015-2024), driven by tourism, remittances, free trade zone manufacturing, and increasingly services exports. GDP per capita has risen from ~$5,200 (2010) to ~$10,800 (2025). Meanwhile, Haiti's GDP per capita has fallen from $720 (2010) to ~$460 (2025) as gang warfare has destroyed economic activity. This divergence — one island, two trajectories separated by 23 years of political violence — creates the unique kill condition gradient. The wider the economic gap, the stronger the migration pull factor. The DR's growth miracle is simultaneously the magnet accelerating KC_01 risk. The BCRD monitors this tension as the defining macro risk of the Caribbean bloc.
