◈ Structural Analysis — Nigeria Q3 2026

KC_01 Single Trigger: Naira Crisis

The naira lost ~50% of its value in June-July 2023 after President Tinubu removed the fuel subsidy and unified exchange rates. USDNGN went from ~460 to ~900 in weeks. The parallel market still trades at a premium (~10-15%) over official. The SINGLE TRIGGER threshold (USDNGN>2000) would represent a further ~25% devaluation from current levels — not the base case but a live tail risk if fiscal or oil stress escalates.

Oil Production & Niger Delta Risk

Nigeria's OPEC+ allocation is 1.8M bpd but actual production hovers at 1.3M bpd — a structural gap driven by pipeline vandalism (bunkering) in the Niger Delta. NNPC estimates 400,000+ bpd is stolen daily via illegal refining. The Dangote refinery (650K bpd capacity) is progressively coming online, potentially reducing fuel import requirements and improving the current account, but timeline slippage remains a risk (KC_03 fiscal upside if Dangote fully operationalises).

West Africa Anchor Risk

Nigeria's instability is not contained — it radiates to Ghana (already in IMF programme), Senegal (political transition), and Côte d'Ivoire (CFA franc pressure). The ECOWAS economic bloc is sensitive to Nigeria's anchor role. US Africa policy (AGOA, Prosper Africa initiative) tracks Nigeria stability as a leading indicator for West Africa investment conditions.

CBN Recapitalisation Deadline

CBN's March 2024 directive requires commercial banks to raise capital: N500B for international banks (e.g., Access Bank, Zenith, GTBank), N200B for national banks by 2026. Rights issues and mergers are underway. Recapitalisation failure at a major bank would trigger ELEVATED KC_05 and spill into broader confidence. Access Bank and GTBank are tracking ahead of schedule; mid-tier banks face more pressure.
