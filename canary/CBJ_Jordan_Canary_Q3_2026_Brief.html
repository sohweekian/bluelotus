◈ Structural Analysis — Jordan Q3 2026

KC_01 SINGLE TRIGGER: West Bank Displacement

Jordan absorbed Palestinian refugees in 1948, 1967, and 1994+. A 4th wave during active West Bank fragmentation is qualitatively different: Jordan's 70%+ Palestinian-origin population creates an internal legitimacy crisis for the Hashemite monarchy. The 1988 Jordan renunciation of the West Bank dissolved formal governance links — a reversal under displacement pressure would reshape the Jordanian state's identity. UNRWA registration already at 2.3M; new arrivals would overwhelm services. KC_01 ≥ 0.85 = CRITICAL, overriding all other kill conditions.

JOD Peg Architecture & IMF Backstop

The JOD/USD peg at 0.709 is one of the most durable pegs in the Middle East, having held since 1995. It rests on three pillars: (1) US budget support ($1.2-1.45B/year); (2) GCC aid ($1.55-1.90B/year, primarily Saudi and UAE); (3) IMF Extended Fund Facility (2024-2027, ~$1.2B). The peg requires ~$4-5B annual external financing given Jordan's persistent current account deficit of ~6% GDP. IMF EFF compliance has deteriorated to 0.79 in Q2 2026. Below 0.80 signals disbursement suspension risk.

Syrian/Iraqi Refugee Architecture

Jordan hosts 660k+ UNHCR-registered Syrian refugees (Zaatari: 76k capacity; Azraq: 36k) and an estimated 65k+ Iraqi refugees. Zaatari camp is near design capacity. WFP Jordan operates a $420M/year food security programme; in Q3 2026, funding gap has forced ration reductions. Syrian HTS territorial consolidation in 2025-2026 reduced return incentives. Any renewed Syria conflict or ISIS resurgence would trigger a new surge. Jordan's social infrastructure — schools, hospitals, water — operates at 130%+ refugee-adjusted capacity.

Wadi Araba Treaty Dynamics (1994)

The Wadi Araba Peace Treaty between Jordan and Israel (October 1994) is the second Arab-Israeli peace treaty after Camp David. It governs water-sharing on the Jordan River, border demarcation, trade, and security cooperation. King Abdullah II has issued multiple public warnings to Israel over Gaza and West Bank operations, including rare direct criticism. The treaty has survived multiple stress tests but its informal suspension (ambassador recall, trade freeze) would immediately trigger a US aid review. A formal treaty suspension = Jordan loses ~$1.45B in annual US support and IMF programme viability collapses.

Water Scarcity: The Structural Accelerant

Jordan is ranked the world's second water-poorest country: <200m³/capita/year versus a global average of ~6,000m³. The Jordan River water-sharing agreement under Wadi Araba allocates 50M cubic metres/year to Jordan from Israel — insufficient and frequently subject to dispute. 2026 per-capita availability: ~190m³ (approaching the 150m³ critical threshold). Climate change is reducing annual rainfall. Syrian upstream water usage on the Yarmouk tributary compounds pressure. Water stress amplifies refugee burden (camp sanitation, agriculture) and acts as a structural multiplier on KC_01 and KC_03.

Hashemite Regime Stability Assessment

King Abdullah II has ruled since 1999 and navigated the Arab Spring, multiple Syria crises, and the Gaza conflict without regime-threatening instability. The Hashemite monarchy derives legitimacy from custodianship of Jerusalem's Islamic holy sites (Waqf) and pan-Arab Hashemite heritage. A Palestinian mass displacement event (KC_01 SINGLE TRIGGER) would uniquely challenge this legitimacy — Jordan's East Bank / West Bank demographic tension would become acute. The regime's repression capability is real but its financial capacity to absorb a 4th refugee wave without foreign support is near zero. US strategic commitment to Jordan (Camp Muwaffaq Salti air base, intelligence sharing) is the critical stabilisation guarantee.
