◈ The Unique Mechanism — "Nationalization Without Development"

Bolivia holds the world's largest lithium reserves — approximately 21 million metric tonnes in the Salar de Uyuni (world's largest salt flat), representing ~42% of global identified lithium resources. Yet Bolivia has failed to monetize this resource for 15+ years due to MAS party state nationalization doctrine (2006). YLB (Yacimientos de Litio Bolivianos), the state lithium company, has been plagued by technical failures, corruption, and political interference. CATL and ACI (Germany) signed JVs in 2023, but YLB's technical capacity is minimal. The result: the world's richest lithium seam producing almost no battery-grade product while the global EV battery industry races ahead.
Meanwhile, Bolivia's natural gas revenue — which funded the Morales boom 2006-2014 — is collapsing as Tarija fields deplete. Gas production peaked at ~60 Mm³/day in 2014 and has fallen to ~40 Mm³/day. Export contracts with Brazil (PETROBRAS, GASBOL pipeline) and Argentina (ENARSA) are near expiry. The BOB (Boliviano) has been pegged at 6.91 BOB/USD since 2012 but BCB foreign reserves have collapsed from $15 billion (2014) to ~$3.4 billion (2026). Without new lithium revenue AND with gas depletion, Bolivia's fiscal model faces structural collapse.

◈ Structural Analysis — Bolivia Q3 2026

KC_01 SINGLE TRIGGER: YLB Lithium / JV Forced Exit

YLB holds 51% in all lithium JVs under Bolivian resource nationalism law. CATL (China) and ACI (Germany) signed JVs in 2023 but YLB's technical capacity for industrial-scale extraction is minimal. Chronic issues: lack of qualified engineers, corruption in procurement, political interference from both MAS factions. If JVs collapse, Bolivia permanently misses the global lithium battery supply chain era while the Salar de Uyuni remains the world's largest untapped lithium reserve. Score: 0.55 (WATCH — approaching SINGLE TRIGGER activation at 0.65).

KC_02: Tarija Gas Field Depletion

Bolivia's gas sector peaked in 2014 at ~60 Mm³/day and has fallen to ~40 Mm³/day by 2026. The Tarija/Chaco fields — which provided the bulk of the Morales boom revenue — are depleting. PETROBRAS (Brazil, GASBOL pipeline) and ENARSA (Argentina) contracts are near expiry. New exploration has been minimal due to state control and fiscal constraints. Gas revenue represented 40%+ of government income at peak; today it is falling sharply. Without replacement revenue (lithium remains zero), fiscal deficit will surge.

KC_03: BOB Peg Stress & Reserve Depletion

The BOB/USD peg at 6.91 has been maintained since 2012, but the BCB has been burning reserves to defend it as hydrocarbon revenues decline. Foreign exchange reserves fell from $15.1B (2014) to approximately $3.4B (2026) — a 78% collapse. The critical threshold is $2B, below which the peg becomes mathematically indefensible without new revenue sources or IMF/external support. A parallel dollar market has begun to emerge in urban centers, signaling early dollarization pressure. Score: 0.50 (approaching $3B watch level).

KC_04: Arce vs Morales — MAS Party War

The ruling MAS party split in 2021 into Arce/García Linera and Morales/Choquehuanca factions. The culmination came in June 2024 when General Juan José Zúñiga led armored vehicles in an assault on Plaza Murillo (the seat of government) in what appeared to be a coup attempt. Morales faces criminal charges (terrorism, statutory rape allegations) but commands significant coca-grower and rural street support. The constitutional crisis risk remains elevated with 2025 elections approaching. Score: 0.45 (WATCH).
