ANALYTICAL ASSESSMENT — Q3 2026 OUTLOOK

Fiscal Dominance (KC_01 — 0.42): Lula's social spending programs continue to pressure the fiscal primary balance. The Selic at 13.25% creates compounding interest burden on the public debt. BCB independence is constitutionally protected but politically contested. The fiscal arcabouço (framework) is struggling to contain expenditure growth. Risk: if market loses confidence in fiscal anchor, BRL depreciation becomes self-reinforcing.

BRL Depreciation (KC_02 — 0.40): USDBRL at ~5.73 sits below the 5.80 alert. The BRL carry trade remains viable at 13.25% Selic vs 4.50% Fed Funds, but the carry premium is compressing. Any EM-wide risk-off event could push USDBRL through 5.80 rapidly. BCB has limited FX reserve ammunition for sustained intervention.

China Demand (KC_03 — 0.35): China iron ore imports running at ~93 Mt/month, -8% YoY. Vale's revenue is tracking below guidance. Soybean demand remains stable as Chinese pork production continues recovery. Risk: China property sector distress amplification could cascade into steeper iron ore decline.

Banking Stress (KC_04 — 0.38): Caixa Econômica Federal and Banco do Brasil carry elevated household NPL ratios. Consumer credit card delinquency above the historical average. BCB systemic risk reporting is WATCH. State bank credit expansion under Lula may be masking underlying NPL deterioration.

Amazon/ESG (KC_05 — 0.40) [UNIQUE]: INPE data confirms deforestation uptick to ~9,200 km² in 2026 vs 8,800 km² in 2025. This has triggered renewed ESG scrutiny. Two major European pension funds placed Brazilian sovereign bonds on ESG review watch. BRL green bond issuance premium widening. If INPE data exceeds 10,000 km², mandatory ESG fund exits become probable, widening Brazil's sovereign spread by an estimated 40-80bps.
