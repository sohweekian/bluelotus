◈ Structural Analysis — Iran Q3 2026

KC_01: Nuclear Enrichment — The Irreversible Threshold

Iran's uranium enrichment at 60%+ places it in striking distance of weapons-grade 90%. The 90% threshold is an irreversible binary event: once crossed, CRITICAL zone is imposed immediately as a single trigger regardless of other KC scores. IAEA access has been progressively restricted since 2021; current access score ~0.40/1.0. NPT withdrawal — as North Korea precedent — would accelerate a 1-2 week weapons assembly timeline. No other CivilisationOS canary has an enrichment-based binary single trigger of this severity.

KC_02: IRGC Proxy Network — The Direct Conflict Trigger

Iran's proxy network encompasses: Houthis (Yemen, Red Sea shipping disruption), Hezbollah (Lebanon, potential northern front against Israel), PMF/PMU (Iraq, US forces pressure), and Syrian IRGC presence. The single trigger fires when IRGC-linked forces directly kill US military personnel, triggering a mandatory escalation to CRITICAL zone. Current activation: Houthis 0.75, Hezbollah 0.60, PMF 0.55 — all elevated but below the direct-conflict threshold.

KC_03: Sanctions Maximum Pressure — The 2026-2027 Snapback

The UN snapback mechanism — embedded in UNSC Resolution 2231 — can be triggered by any JCPOA participant, restoring all pre-2015 UN sanctions. With the JCPOA effectively collapsed, snapback risk is acute. Iran currently exports ~1.5M bbl/day through China-routed evasion schemes (shadow fleet, ship-to-ship transfers). If secondary sanctions enforcement against Iranian oil buyers (particularly Chinese refineries) is activated, exports could collapse to below 500k bbl/day — the CRITICAL threshold for this kill condition.

KC_04 & KC_05: IRR Spiral & Hormuz Risk

The IRR has lost 99.97% of its value since the 1979 revolution. The parallel market rate (~580k IRR/USD) is maintained by a dual-rate system upheld by capital controls; breach of 700k/USD would signal capital control failure and bank run risk. The Strait of Hormuz carries 20% of global oil daily (21M bbl/day). Iran mined Hormuz during the 1987-88 Tanker War and retains this capability. Current risk: rhetoric elevated at 0.30 score (threat-only). Lloyd's war-risk insurance premiums are the leading indicator of imminent closure.

◈ Inverse Bullish Logic — FSLR / ENPH / TAN

When the Strait of Hormuz closes or Iran-driven oil shock materializes, the energy crisis narrative dramatically accelerates political and policy urgency for domestic clean energy adoption in the US. First Solar (FSLR), as a US domestic solar manufacturer benefiting from IRA Section 45X tax credits, gains from the "energy independence" emergency narrative.
