◈ The Georgia Unique Mechanism — Why This Canary Matters

KC_01 SINGLE TRIGGER — Russian Occupation

Russia has occupied ~20% of Georgian territory since the August 2008 war. South Ossetia (capital: Tskhinvali) and Abkhazia are administered by Russian-backed authorities with Russian military bases inside internationally recognized Georgian borders. 'Borderization' — the gradual movement of barbed-wire fence lines deeper into Georgian territory — is an ongoing process documented by the EU Monitoring Mission. The 2014 Crimea and 2022 Ukraine playbooks are directly applicable. One Russian military order fires KC_01 → GERI → CRITICAL instantly.

EU Accession Freeze & Georgian Dream

Georgia applied for EU membership in March 2022, received candidate status in December 2023. But in May 2024, the Georgian Dream government — widely accused of pro-Russian orientation — passed a "Foreign Agents Law" modelled on Russian legislation requiring civil society organizations receiving foreign funding to register as "agents of foreign influence." The EU responded by freezing accession negotiations. Mass protests in Tbilisi (dubbed the "Russian Law" protests) were met with police crackdowns. KC_02 sits at 0.50 — the freeze continues.

BTC Pipeline — Caspian Energy Chokepoint

The Baku-Tbilisi-Ceyhan (BTC) pipeline is the primary export route for Azerbaijani and Kazakhstani crude oil (~1M bbl/day), operated by BP. It crosses Georgia for ~250km, running through contested territory near South Ossetia. The 2008 August War briefly threatened the pipeline. PKK (Turkey) and IRGC (Iran) have targeted BTC in previous incidents. KC_04 monitors security posture. BP (operator) is BEARISH; XOM/CVX are BULLISH on supply reduction.

Russian Expat Paradox — GEL Strength (KC_03)

Post-February 2022, Georgia paradoxically benefited from Russia's invasion of Ukraine: hundreds of thousands of Russian expats (tech workers, anti-war activists, draft-avoiders) relocated to Tbilisi, bringing capital, remittances, and tech industry activity. This strengthened the GEL (USDGEL briefly hit ~2.45 from ~2.85). However, this dependency is a structural fragility: if sanctions tighten (Russian cards blocked), geopolitical rapprochement reverses, or a second Russian mobilization triggers outmigration, KC_03 reverses sharply.
