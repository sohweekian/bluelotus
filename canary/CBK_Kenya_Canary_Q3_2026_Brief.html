EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

EAST AFRICA HUB STATUS

Kenya remains Sub-Saharan Africa's premier technology and fintech hub with Google, Microsoft, and Meta operating African headquarters from Nairobi. M-Pesa processes approximately $300B annually serving 95%+ of Kenyan adults. The KEKRI at 0.3820 WATCH reflects a stable but monitored risk environment following the resolution of the February 2024 Eurobond refinancing crisis and the July 2024 Gen Z protest wave.

UNIQUE KC01: CLIMATE AS MONETARY RISK

Kenya is the only CivilisationOS canary monitoring climate as a primary monetary kill condition. Agriculture constitutes 33% of GDP and employs 60% of the labour force. Hydroelectric power (40-70% of Kenya Power grid) is fully rain-dependent. ENSO-driven drought cycles — including the worst Horn of Africa drought in 40 years (2021-2023) — create direct fiscal shocks requiring emergency spending up to $2.6B. Current climate index: 0.38 WATCH.

EUROBOND & SOVEREIGN DEBT (KC03 SINGLE TRIGGER)

Kenya successfully refinanced its $2B Eurobond in February 2024 via a $1.5B new issuance at 10.375% plus IMF and bilateral support. Debt/GDP ratio remains elevated at ~65% with debt service consuming ~35% of revenue. An active IMF Extended Fund Facility (EFF) provides external anchor. Any missed sovereign payment triggers a CRITICAL minimum regardless of composite KEKRI score. Current Eurobond score: 0.35 WATCH.

KES & POLITICAL RISK

USDKES at ~130 is near the 130 watch threshold after recovering from the January 2024 record low of 160+. CBK holds approximately 4 months of import cover. The Gen Z protest movement forced withdrawal of Finance Bill 2024, creating a fiscal revenue shortfall and increased borrowing pressure. Remittances ($4.6B/yr) remain the primary FX buffer supporting KES stability.

Score 0.30 reflects managed security risk with heightened vigilance in North Kenya and Nairobi.
