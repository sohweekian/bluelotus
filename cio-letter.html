Why Superforecasting changes our approach

Philip Tetlock's research across 25,000 forecasters established that the most accurate predictors are not those with the most information — they are those who express beliefs probabilistically, decompose complex questions systematically, and update continuously on new evidence. Applied to equity markets: instead of asking "will BAC rise?", we ask "what is the probability BAC exceeds $62.46 within 90 days, and what inputs drive that estimate?"

The Brier Score measures our calibration over time. The Kelly Criterion sizes each position proportional to our genuine edge. Thorp's margin of safety provides the final conservative anchor. Together, these three pillars — Tetlock, Kelly, Thorp — form the secret sauce of the BlueLotus investment process.

The 4-Step Independent Valuation Method

BlueLotus Superforecasting Valuation Protocol
① 8-Lens Evidence Aggregation — Each of the 8 lenses contributes an independent signal: bullish, bearish, or neutral. Contradictions between lenses (L1 Fundamental vs L7 Institutional) are treated as information, not noise.
② DCF or Earnings-Based Base Valuation — Profitable companies: EPS × sector P/E. Loss-making companies: revenue run-rate → EBITDA projection → EV/EBITDA multiple → per-share value.
③ Macro and Strategic Adjustments — Apply the dominant macro force (rate hike NIM expansion for banks; critical minerals premium for rare earths). Apply geopolitical discount for tail risk exposure.
④ Thorp Margin of Safety — Apply 5–10% conservative discount to the adjusted target. This is not pessimism — it is mathematical humility. Never pay for perfection.
